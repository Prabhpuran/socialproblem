mental tips


1

Set Realistic Goals and Deadlines

Avoid overwhelming yourself with high expectations. Break projects into manageable tasks with achievable timelines.

2

Create a Routine

A consistent writing schedule helps build discipline and keeps you from feeling burnt out. Set specific times for writing, breaks, and relaxation.

3

Take Breaks and Move

Writing often means long hours sitting. Every hour or so, take short breaks to stretch, walk, or do simple exercises. Moving around improves focus and reduces fatigue.

4

Stay Connected with Other Writers

Join writing groups, whether in person or online. These can offer feedback, encouragement, and camaraderie, which helps combat isolation.

5

Practice Self-Compassion

Writing can be challenging, so be kind to yourself when you hit a block or don't reach your goals. Recognize your efforts and progress.

6

Limit Social Media During Writing Sessions

Social media can distract and drain energy. Try using apps that block or limit social media usage during your writing time.

7

Use Mindfulness and Meditation

Practicing mindfulness or meditation can improve concentration and reduce anxiety. Apps like Headspace or Calm are helpful for quick sessions.
